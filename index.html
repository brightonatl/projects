Welcome to the Brighton School Atal Tinkering Lab repository dashboard. We have two key project assessment divisions structured to test conceptual mapping and physical engineering realization.

Part 1: Idea Mapping

Minor Projects (15M)

Minor projects are conceptual setups where students are tested on their ideas and block layouts. There is no physical component build required at this stage. Submissions must be compiled and submitted purely in softcopy formats.

Submission Instructions:

  • Prepare your proposal idea in PPT, PDF, or DOCX format.
  • Fill in the official Google Form provided in your minor project file instructions.
  • Either upload the softcopy via the form OR hand over the file directly to your respected Robotics Teacher.

Rubric Weightage Break-down:

3M Idea / Novelty
6M Circuit Flow
3M Teacher Viva
3M Softcopy Report

The Transition Rule IMPORTANT

Students must transition from Minor to Major with the same idea. The exact concept submitted in your Minor Project softcopy must now be transformed into a functional, physical reality in the ATL Lab.

Concept Physical Build
Part 2: Hardware Prototyping

Major Projects (20M)

In this division, you are building the physical model of the idea you previously pitched. Evaluate component connections, construct your code structure, and calibrate sensors securely. You will present a fully functioning hardware project during evaluation.

Team Identity: Keep the same team members declared in the Minor softcopy.
Inventory Desk: Reference your Costing table to request modules and sensors from lab coordinators.
Final Deliverables: A physical, functioning model, live demo testing runs, and clean wiring.

Rubric Weightage Break-down:

4M Problem Depth
6M Hardware Build
4M System Testing
6M Practical Demonstration
